# Overview

Vannevar Bush (1890–1974) was an American engineer and science administrator. In his 1945 essay "As We May Think," he proposed the **Memex** — a theoretical device for personal knowledge storage with associative trails between documents. This concept is widely regarded as a precursor to hypermedia and personal knowledge management systems.

# Key Facts

- Published "As We May Think" in 1945, proposing the Memex
- Memex vision: personal, curated knowledge store with associative connections
- Bush's vision was closer to personal knowledge management than what the web became
- The unsolved problem in Bush's vision: who does the maintenance? [LLMs solve this](/concepts/llm-wiki-pattern.md).
