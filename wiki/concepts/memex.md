# Overview

The Memex ("memory extender") was proposed by [Vannevar Bush](/entities/vannevar-bush.md) in his 1945 essay "As We May Think." It was conceived as a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents — closer to personal knowledge management than what the web eventually became.

Bush's vision had one unsolved problem: **who does the maintenance?** Building and maintaining associative trails between documents is labor-intensive. Humans abandon knowledge bases because the maintenance burden grows faster than the value. The [LLM Wiki Pattern](/concepts/llm-wiki-pattern.md) solves this — LLMs handle the maintenance at near-zero cost.

# Key Points

- Proposed in 1945 by Vannevar Bush
- Personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails
- Vision was closer to personal PKM than the public web
- Unsolved problem: maintenance burden — solved by LLMs in the LLM Wiki Pattern
- Spirit lives on in tools like Obsidian and patterns like the LLM Wiki
